More Than Sad: Suicide Awareness Training

Please be patient while the page is loading.

Event Details:

In an American Foundation for Suicide Prevention 2020 report, suicide is identified as the 2nd leading cause of death for Arizona's adolescents and young adults (ages 10-34). In fact, on average, one person dies by suicide every six hours in our state.

As an intervention to reduce the suicide rates across the state, the legislature passed a law that, as of July 1, 2020, all school staff in grades 6 through 12 must have suicide awareness training at least once every three years from one of eight approved curriculums.

Participants in this class will increase their understanding of the problem of youth suicide, the risk factors that lead to suicide, and the treatment and prevention of suicidal behavior in adolescents.

  • This course meets the Arizona legislative requirements for mandatory training.
  • This course is for Central Campus Employees
